I'm an engineer who is interested in driving AI into medicine.

My Mission

To leverage artificial intelligence - particularly machine learning - to improve medicine through the use of assistive decision-making software, patient-specific predictive models, and streamlined workflow tools.

I am interested in bringing new technologies to medicine. Over time, my research interests have transitioned from basic biological research into applied computational areas. I began with genetic engineering and stem cell & neural engineering before moving into machine learning and mechanistic modeling.

My previous projects focused on developing ensemble methods for nanomaterial optimization, implementing image recognition techniques for medical annotation, and building multimodal neural networks for predicting patient outcomes. At the NIH, I will be using 3D electron microscopy to study the structure of thrombi to better understand the blood clotting process, as well as examining platelets from COVID-19 hospitalized patients to understand the cause for abnormal clotting.
